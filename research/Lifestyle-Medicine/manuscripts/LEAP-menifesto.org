

* LEAP Philosphy
LifeQuality Evidence for Action Partnership: Conceptual Foundation and Research Agenda

Vision
To create an open partnership through which people, professionals, researchers and communities work together to ask meaningful questions, build trustworthy evidence, translate evidence into informed action, and continuously learn together to improve LifeQuality.

Why LEAP?
The twenty-first century has produced unprecedented scientific knowledge about healthy living, yet chronic lifestyle-related diseases continue to rise across the world.
The challenge facing lifestyle medicine is no longer simply discovering what promotes health.
The challenge is enabling people to translate existing evidence into sustained action that improves health, wellbeing and quality of life.

Central Hypothesis
The persistent Evidence-to-Health Gap arises not only from limitations in evidence generation, health systems or social determinants, but also from insufficient partnership with people throughout the continuum from problem definition to evidence generation, evidence translation, informed action and continuous shared learning.

The LEAP Philosophy
Health is not delivered to people.
It is co-created through partnerships in which scientific evidence is openly shared, collectively interpreted and responsibly applied to improve quality of life.

Scientific evidence does not create health.
People create health.
Evidence helps people create health.

The purpose of scientific evidence is not merely to advance knowledge, but to enable informed action that improves people’s quality of life.

People are not passive recipients of health advice.
They are partners in asking meaningful questions, generating better evidence, translating that evidence into understandable knowledge, applying it in their own lives and sharing what they learn to improve future evidence.

Five Foundational Principles
1. People are central.
People, families and communities are the primary creators and maintainers of health.

2. Evidence remains scientific.
Scientific inquiry, critical appraisal and transparent synthesis remain the foundation of trustworthy knowledge.

3. Evidence should be translated for people.
Scientific evidence should be systematically synthesized into forms that are openly accessible, understandable, relatable and useful in people’s own circumstances.
Evidence-informed options are one practical output of this translation.

4. Health is collaboratively created.
Health is built through partnerships among people, professionals, communities and institutions.

5. Rights and responsibilities are inseparable.
Meaningful participation naturally entails meaningful ownership.
Partnership strengthens both autonomy and responsibility.

LEAP
LifeQuality Evidence for Action Partnership
LEAP is an open partnership that enables people, families, communities, professionals and researchers to ask meaningful questions, discover trustworthy answers through rigorous scientific evidence, translate that evidence into forms that are openly accessible, understandable and relevant to people’s lives, and support informed action based on the shared responsibility each of us bears for building health and improving LifeQuality.
Through continuous reflection, collaboration and shared learning, LEAP seeks to strengthen both scientific knowledge and people’s capacity to create healthier, happier and longer lives.

LEAP does not seek to replace existing institutions, disciplines or professional expertise. It seeks to strengthen them by creating better partnerships between science and society.
