# Practice Knowledge System (PKS)

# Editorial Guide v1.0

**Status:** Governing Document

**Applies to:** All contributors, editors, reviewers, and AI-assisted content generation within the Practice Knowledge System.

---

# 1. Purpose

This Editorial Guide establishes the editorial standards governing the Practice Knowledge System (PKS).

Its objective is to ensure that all published content is:

* scientifically accurate
* evidence-based
* practically useful
* transparent
* consistent
* regularly updated

This guide complements the public Editorial Charter and provides operational guidance for developing and maintaining content.

---

# 2. Editorial Mission

The Practice Knowledge System exists to translate the best available scientific evidence into practical knowledge that improves health.

Every page should help readers answer one practical question:

> **How can the best available evidence be applied to improve health?**

---

# 3. Intended Audience

Content should be written for one or more of the following audiences:

* Patients
* Caregivers
* Healthcare professionals
* Medical students
* Researchers
* Public health professionals
* Policymakers

Whenever appropriate, identify the intended audience at the beginning of each page.

---

# 4. Editorial Principles

Every page should satisfy the following principles.

## Evidence First

Recommendations should be based upon the highest available quality of evidence.

Preferred hierarchy:

1. Clinical Practice Guidelines
2. Systematic Reviews
3. Meta-analyses
4. Randomized Controlled Trials
5. Observational Studies
6. Expert Opinion

---

## Practical Relevance

Readers should understand:

* what is known
* what remains uncertain
* how evidence may reasonably be applied

---

## Transparency

Scientific uncertainty should be acknowledged openly.

Where evidence is weak or conflicting, this should be stated explicitly.

---

## Neutrality

Avoid promotional language.

Avoid overstating benefits.

Present both benefits and limitations.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Medical knowledge evolves continuously.

Pages should undergo periodic review and updating.

# 5. Content Categories

The Practice Knowledge System currently includes:

* Health Topics
* Lifestyle Interventions
* Evidence Library
* Clinical Guides
* Learning Resources
* Research Connections

Each category follows a standard template.

# 6. Standard Templates

## Disease Pages

Recommended structure:

* Summary
* Key Messages
* Overview
* Causes
* Risk Factors
* Symptoms
* Diagnosis
* Lifestyle Interventions
* Evidence Summary
* Clinical Practice
* Frequently Asked Questions
* References

---

## Lifestyle Intervention Pages

Recommended structure:

* Overview
* Mechanisms
* Evidence
* Benefits
* Contraindications
* Practical Guidance
* References

---

## Evidence Briefs

Recommended structure:

* Clinical Question
* Bottom Line
* Key Findings
* Quality of Evidence
* Practical Implications
* References

---

## Practice Perspectives

Recommended structure:

* Background
* Practical Experience
* Lessons Learned
* Implementation Challenges
* Key Messages

Practice Perspectives should distinguish clearly between published evidence and implementation experience.

---

# 7. Writing Style

Write using:

* plain English
* short paragraphs
* descriptive headings
* concise sentences
* active voice where appropriate

Avoid:

* sensational language
* unsupported opinions
* unnecessary jargon
* excessive marketing language

---

# 8. References

Preferred citation style:

**Vancouver**

Primary literature should be cited whenever possible.

Avoid citing secondary summaries when the original publication is available.

---

# 9. Evidence Confidence

Whenever recommendations are made, indicate the confidence of supporting evidence.

Suggested categories:

* 🟢 High
* 🟡 Moderate
* 🟠 Limited
* 🔴 Insufficient

---

# 10. Images and Multimedia

Images should support learning.

Appropriate examples include:

* anatomical diagrams
* educational illustrations
* exercise demonstrations
* explanatory figures

Avoid decorative images without educational value.

---

# 11. Patient Stories

Patient experiences should illustrate implementation rather than prove effectiveness.

Requirements include:

* informed consent
* protection of privacy
* removal of identifying information where appropriate

Testimonials should not substitute for scientific evidence.

---

# 12. Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence may assist with:

* drafting
* summarisation
* language improvement
* formatting
* literature organisation

Human editors remain responsible for:

* scientific accuracy
* interpretation
* final approval

AI-generated content should be reviewed before publication.

---

# 13. Editorial Workflow

Every page should follow this sequence:

1. Topic selection
2. Literature review
3. Evidence appraisal
4. Draft preparation
5. Editorial review
6. Medical review (where appropriate)
7. Publication
8. Scheduled review and update

---

# 14. Metadata

Each published page should include, where appropriate:

* Author
* Reviewer
* Date Created
* Date Updated
* Intended Audience
* Reading Time
* Evidence Confidence

---

# 15. Conflicts of Interest

Authors and reviewers should disclose relevant financial or non-financial conflicts of interest.

Where none exist, state:

> No conflicts of interest declared.

---

# 16. Version Control

Substantive editorial revisions should be recorded through Git commits with clear commit messages.

Major changes to editorial policy should be documented in the project changelog.

---

# 17. Relationship to the LEAP Ecosystem

The Practice Knowledge System forms one component of the broader LEAP ecosystem.

* **lifequality.org.in** — community programmes, education, outreach, and organisational activities.
* **practice.lifequality.org.in** — translation of evidence into practical health guidance.
* **academic.lifequality.org.in** — research, publications, scholarly perspectives, and scientific discourse.

Content should remain aligned with the role of the Practice Knowledge System and avoid duplication across the ecosystem.
