# Knowledge Lifecycle in the Practice Knowledge System (PKS)

Version: 1.0
Status: Draft

---

# Purpose

Knowledge within the Practice Knowledge System (PKS) is viewed as a continuously evolving process rather than a collection of static documents.

Scientific evidence evolves through observation, inquiry, research, synthesis, application, evaluation and continuous learning. The role of PKS is to support this complete lifecycle by connecting research, practice and people's lived experiences into a continuously learning ecosystem.

The Knowledge Lifecycle operationalises the LEAP philosophy by ensuring that every stage of knowledge creation contributes to improved health and LifeQuality.

# Stage 1 — Questions

Knowledge begins with meaningful questions.

Questions may arise from

- individuals
- communities
- clinicians
- researchers
- policymakers
- public health agencies

PKS encourages documenting unanswered questions before evidence exists.

Output

Research Questions

Research Priorities

Evidence Gaps

---

# Stage 2 — Research

Research produces scientific knowledge using rigorous and transparent methods.

Outputs include

- Clinical trials
- Systematic reviews
- Meta-analyses
- Qualitative studies
- Implementation studies
- Citizen science
- Community participatory research

PKS does not replace scientific publishing.

It connects scientific publishing to practical knowledge.

---

# Stage 3 — Evidence

Evidence is critically appraised and synthesised.

Evidence should remain

- transparent
- reproducible
- openly referenced

Outputs include

Evidence Summaries

Evidence Maps

Guidelines

Living Reviews

---

# Stage 4 — Translation

Evidence is translated into forms that are

- accessible
- understandable
- relatable
- actionable

Translation should preserve scientific integrity while improving usability.

Outputs include

Practice Guides

Decision Aids

Patient Resources

Infographics

Educational Materials

---

# Stage 5 — Practice

Knowledge becomes valuable only when used.

Practice occurs in

- healthcare
- homes
- schools
- workplaces
- communities

Health is ultimately created in people's daily lives.

---

# Stage 6 — Outcomes

PKS recognises multiple outcomes.

Clinical outcomes

Behavioural outcomes

Participation

Health

LifeQuality

Equity

Knowledge itself is not the endpoint.

Improved LifeQuality is.

---

# Stage 7 — Learning

Implementation generates new knowledge.

Learning may identify

- unexpected benefits
- barriers
- contextual factors
- new research questions
- evidence gaps

Every implementation becomes an opportunity for learning.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Knowledge objects remain living resources.

Updates occur whenever

new evidence

better synthesis

editorial improvements

community feedback

scientific correction

become available.

Version history is preserved.

Nothing is overwritten.

---

# Roles

Researchers

Generate evidence.

Healthcare professionals

Communities

Provide context.

Individuals

Create health through daily life.

PKS

Connects every stage into a continuously learning system.

---

# Relationship to LEAP

The Knowledge Lifecycle operationalises the Learning and Evidence for Action and Practice (LEAP) framework.

LEAP provides the conceptual foundation.

The Knowledge Lifecycle provides the operational workflow.

Together they connect

questions

↓

evidence

↓

knowledge

↓

practice

↓

health

↓

LifeQuality

↓

new questions

creating a continuously learning ecosystem.

---

# Guiding Principle

Knowledge is not complete when it is published.

Knowledge fulfils its purpose when it contributes to better LifeQuality and generates better questions.
