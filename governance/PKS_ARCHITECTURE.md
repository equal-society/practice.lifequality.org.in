## Architecture

Version: 1.0
Status: Draft

---

# Vision

The Practice Knowledge System (PKS) is an open, continuously learning knowledge ecosystem designed to translate scientific evidence into improved health and LifeQuality.

PKS recognises that health is ultimately created in people's daily lives. Scientific evidence, healthcare professionals, health systems and communities contribute to this process by strengthening people's capacity to create, preserve and restore health.

The system therefore organises knowledge around partnership rather than disciplines.

---

# Core Principles

1. Evidence before opinion
2. Partnership before prescription
3. Health creation rather than healthcare delivery
4. Open science
5. Continuous learning
6. Versioned knowledge
7. Transparency
8. Attribution
9. Interoperability
10. Automation wherever possible

# Versioning

Every knowledge object has

• Identifier

• Version

• Status

• Revision history

Knowledge evolves continuously.

Nothing is ever "finished."

---

# Relationships

Knowledge objects are connected through metadata rather than manual hyperlinks.

Examples

Foundation ↔ Health Topic

Health Topic ↔ Intervention

Intervention ↔ Evidence

Evidence ↔ Practice Guide

Practice Guide ↔ Patient Resource

Research ↔ Knowledge Gap
