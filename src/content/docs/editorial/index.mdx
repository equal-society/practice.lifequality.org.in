# Editorial Charter

## Our Mission

The **Practice Knowledge System (PKS)** exists to translate the best available scientific evidence into practical knowledge that supports better health.

Our mission is to bridge the gap between research and real-world practice by producing evidence-informed, transparent, and practical educational resources for clinicians, students, patients, caregivers, researchers, and the public.

PKS is an educational resource and is not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

---

## Our Editorial Principles

Every page published on this platform is guided by the following principles.

### Evidence First

Scientific evidence forms the foundation of every recommendation.

Where possible, priority is given to:

1. Clinical practice guidelines
2. Systematic reviews
3. Meta-analyses
4. Randomized controlled trials
5. High-quality observational studies

When evidence is limited or uncertain, this is stated clearly.

---

### Practice-Oriented

Knowledge should be useful.

Our objective is not merely to summarise research but to explain how evidence may be translated into everyday clinical and lifestyle practice.

---

### Transparency

Scientific uncertainty is an essential part of healthcare.

Where evidence is conflicting, evolving, or insufficient, this is openly acknowledged.

Readers should always understand the level of confidence behind recommendations.

---

### Neutrality

PKS does not promote products, commercial services, or unsupported health claims.

Recommendations are based on available scientific evidence rather than opinion or marketing.

---

### Continuous Improvement

Medical knowledge evolves continuously.

Pages are reviewed and updated periodically as new evidence becomes available.

---

## Evidence Confidence

Recommendations are presented with an indication of the strength of supporting evidence whenever appropriate.

| Confidence      | Interpretation                                                       |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 🟢 High         | Supported by multiple high-quality guidelines or systematic reviews. |
| 🟡 Moderate     | Supported by good evidence with some limitations.                    |
| 🟠 Limited      | Evidence is emerging or inconsistent.                                |
| 🔴 Insufficient | Current evidence is inadequate for firm conclusions.                 |

---

## Content Development

Each resource follows a structured editorial process:

1. Literature review
2. Evidence appraisal
3. Draft preparation
4. Medical review
5. Editorial review
6. Publication
7. Periodic update

---

## Intended Audience

The Practice Knowledge System is designed for:

* Patients and caregivers
* Clinicians
* Medical and allied health students
* Public health professionals
* Researchers
* Policymakers

---

## Independence

Editorial decisions are based on scientific merit and public health relevance.

Commercial interests do not determine the content of this platform.

Any conflicts of interest or funding relevant to individual publications will be declared.

---

## Use of Artificial Intelligence

Artificial intelligence may assist with literature organisation, drafting, language editing, formatting, and technical workflows.

All published content remains subject to human editorial oversight, critical appraisal, and scientific review.

Responsibility for published material rests with the editorial team.

Our goal is not simply to disseminate information, but to help transform evidence into better decisions, better practice, and better health.
